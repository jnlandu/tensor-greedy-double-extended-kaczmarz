\section{Abstract}
\begin{frame}{Abstract}
Tensors are intensively used to represent images, videos, and any higher dimensional object. In image reconstruction, given a blurred and noisy images, the reconstruction of the image can be expressed as a tensor linear systems. In this note, we present a tensor greedy double extended Kaczmarz (TGDBEK) for solving large scale tensor linear systems. Unlike the block-based methods TREBK, TREGBK, TREABK encountered in the literature which more or less require a pred-defined partition of the tensor system, the TGDBEK \textbf{does not require any pre-defined partitions of the tensor systems}. It employs \textbf{the greedy principle on the tensor systems to dynamically construct a relatively small subset of rows and columns slices}. Then updates the iterates  using  a REK update style.  We provide the convergence guarantees of the proposed method. We have sown that the proposed method converges exponentially in expectation to the minimum-norm solutions. Numerical examples are conducted to asses the effectiveness of the proposed method.
\end{frame}
